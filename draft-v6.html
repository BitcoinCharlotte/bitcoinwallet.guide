Common Misconceptions
"It's used by criminals"
Less than 2% of crypto activity is illicit. Banks have paid $321B in fines since 2008. HSBC laundered $881M for cartels. Bitcoin is more transparent — every transaction is recorded publicly, forever.
"It uses too much energy"
That energy is what makes Bitcoin real. Proof of work ties Bitcoin to the physical world — real electricity, real hardware, real cost. That's what makes it secure and impossible to fake. It also incentivizes renewable energy and stabilizes power grids.
"It's too volatile"
There's no path from $0 to global money without volatility. Adoption follows: store of value → medium of exchange → unit of account. The volatility has been overwhelmingly upward.
"The government will ban it"
China tried in 2021. The network never stopped. Miners moved to the US. El Salvador embraced it — 30% tourism growth, double-digit GDP. Countries that ban it lose. Countries that adopt it win.
"It's a Ponzi scheme"
No leader, no company, no promises of returns, no pre-mine. Open source code anyone can audit. The most transparent financial system ever built.
"Quantum computers will break it"
Today's best: 105 qubits. Breaking Bitcoin requires millions — decades away. Post-quantum upgrades are already planned (BIP 360). In 1995, they said video would break the internet.
"Another crypto will replace it"
Bitcoin is grounded in physical reality through proof of work — real energy, real cost. Other cryptos use proof of stake, which Ethereum's own creator called "a simulated universe with its own laws of physics."
"You can't touch it"
That's the point. You can't — and neither can a bank, a government, or anyone else. Your time. Your energy. Your rules.

The Root Cause
Money is stored time and energy. You go to work, trade your hours and effort, and receive money. That money is supposed to represent your life's work. When governments print more of it, they are stealing your time.

In 1971, the US severed the dollar's link to gold. For the first time in history, money had no anchor to anything scarce.

Governments gained a "Big Red Button" — the power to print unlimited money. And they can't stop pressing it.

  • The dollar has lost over 87% of its purchasing power since 1971
  • 40% of all dollars ever created were printed in 2020–2022
  • Every new dollar devalues your savings — a hidden tax called inflation
Who benefits? Those closest to the money printer spend new dollars before prices rise. Everyone else pays the bill. This is the Cantillon Effect.
1971: A house cost 2.5× the average salary.
2024: A house costs 8.6× the average salary.
Your time buys less every year — by design.

War: Governments fund endless conflicts by printing money — no need to raise taxes or ask permission. Sound money forces hard choices; fiat writes blank checks.

Obesity: Subsidized corn, soy, and seed oils make junk food cheaper than real food. The subsidies are funded by printed money. The food that's killing us is the food that's cheapest — by design.

Addiction & Despair: When working harder buys less every year, people lose hope. Opioids, alcohol, screens — they're coping mechanisms for a system that quietly steals your future.

The money is broken. Everything else is a symptom.
